# Your First Month in RTP

You've survived week one. Now let's get properly settled.

---

## 1. Get Your NC Driver's License

**NC DMV (NCDMV)** — You'll need to convert your home country license to a North Carolina one.

**Required documents for NC DL:**
- Passport + valid visa
- I-94 (print from i94.cbp.dhs.gov)
- SSN (or denial letter if not yet received)
- Two proofs of NC residency (utility bill + lease agreement)

**Convenient DMV locations:**
- **Cary:** 131 SW Cary Pkwy — generally less busy than Raleigh
- **Morrisville:** 1340 Morrisville Pkwy
- **Raleigh North:** 4121 New Bern Ave

📅 **Make an appointment:** ncdot.gov/dmv — walk-ins wait 2–3 hours. Appointments are much faster.

**Written test:** 25 questions on NC traffic laws. Study at ncdot.gov/dmv/license-id/nc-driver-handbook.

> **International license holders:** NC accepts foreign licenses for up to 1 year. You can drive legally while waiting for your NC license.

## 2. Find Permanent Housing

**Common areas where Bangladeshis live:**
- **Morrisville** — closest to RTP tech park, excellent schools, large South Asian community
- **Cary** — very safe, excellent Wake County schools (especially near Kildaire Farm Rd area)
- **North Raleigh** — more affordable, near NCSU
- **Durham** — diverse, near Duke, more affordable

**Tips:**
- Budget: 1BR apartments run $1,400–$1,900/month in Cary/Morrisville
- Ask in community Facebook groups for referrals — community members often share leads
- **Don't sign a lease without visiting** — photos can be misleading
- Renter's insurance is cheap (~$15/month) and often required

**Bangla-speaking real estate agents:** See [Community Directory](/directory) for agents who know the area well.

## 3. Enroll Children in School

NC public schools are excellent and free.

**Wake County Schools (Cary, Morrisville, Raleigh):**  
📞 (919) 431-0000  
🌐 wcpss.net/enrollment  
📍 Enrollment office: 5625 Dillard Drive, Cary, NC 27518

**Durham Public Schools:**  
📞 (919) 560-2000  
🌐 dpsnc.net

**What to bring for enrollment:**
- Proof of residency (lease + utility bill)
- Immunization records (NC has specific requirements)
- Previous school records/transcripts
- Birth certificate
- Passport/visa

> **ELL Services:** Both school districts provide excellent English Language Learning support. Your child will be assessed and placed in appropriate ELL classes.

## 4. Set Up Utilities

**Electric:**
- **Duke Energy** (most of Wake/Durham counties) — duke-energy.com or 1-800-777-9898
- **Dominion Energy** (some areas) — dominionenergy.com

**Internet:**
- **Spectrum** — most widely available, ~$50/month
- **AT&T Fiber** — faster, available in many neighborhoods, ~$55/month
- **Google Fiber** — available in parts of RTP/Durham, ~$70/month (fastest)

**Gas (if applicable):**
- **Piedmont Natural Gas** — piedmontng.com

> You'll need your SSN or deposit to set up utilities. New arrivals often need to pay a $200–$300 deposit if no credit history.

## 5. Get Health Insurance

**Through Employer (H-1B workers):**  
Enroll during your company's open enrollment or within 30 days of hire. Common plans: BlueCross BlueShield NC, Aetna, United Healthcare.

**Students (F-1 visa):**  
NC State, UNC, and Duke all offer student health insurance plans. Check your university's student health services.

**Uninsured / Between Jobs:**  
- Healthcare.gov marketplace plans
- **Community Health Center of the Eno** (Durham) offers sliding scale fees
- **Advance Community Health** (Raleigh) — low-cost primary care

**Finding a Bangla-speaking doctor:**  
Check our [Community Directory](/directory) for Bengali-speaking physicians in the area.

---

## 6. Buy a Car

Public transportation in the Triangle is limited. A car is almost essential.

**Tips:**
- Without a credit history, expect to pay higher interest rates or put 20%+ down
- **CarMax** and **AutoTrader** are good starting points
- Community Facebook groups often have Bangladeshi families selling cars
- **Insurance:** Progressive, Geico, and State Farm are popular. Expect $150–$250/month for new drivers without US history

---

## 7. Set Up Credit History

US credit history is crucial for apartments, cars, and eventually mortgages.

**Start immediately:**
1. **Secured credit card** — most banks offer these; you deposit $500 and get a card to build credit
2. **Credit builder loan** — some credit unions (like Local Government Federal Credit Union) offer these
3. **Become an authorized user** — a US-citizen friend can add you to their card
